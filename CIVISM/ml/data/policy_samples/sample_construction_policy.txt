SECTION 1: OPERATIONAL TIMINGS

1.1 Approved Working Hours
Construction work is allowed from 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM on weekdays. Work on public holidays is prohibited unless classified as essential infrastructure.

1.2 Extended & Special Hours
Extended working hours may be granted for time-sensitive public projects after review. Any work beyond approved hours must follow additional noise and safety controls.

SECTION 2: WORKER SAFETY & SITE MANAGEMENT

2.1 Safety Audits
All sites must undergo bi-weekly safety audits conducted by certified inspectors. Critical operations such as excavation and lifting require pre-task safety clearance.

2.2 Protective Measures
Workers must be equipped with task-specific protective gear including helmets, gloves, safety harnesses, and eye protection. Safety compliance is the responsibility of the contractor.

2.3 Incident Management
All sites must maintain an on-site first-aid unit and emergency contact list. Any accident must be reported to authorities within 24 hours.

SECTION 3: AREA-BASED GUIDELINES

3.1 Housing & Community Areas
Projects near residential communities must follow strict noise and vibration limits. Construction vehicles must adhere to designated routes to reduce congestion.

3.2 Industrial & Business Districts
Construction in industrial and business zones may operate longer hours with prior approval. Adequate lighting and traffic signage are mandatory.

SECTION 4: ENVIRONMENTAL PROTECTION MEASURES

4.1 Noise Control Standards
Use of low-noise machinery is encouraged. Temporary sound insulation must be installed near schools, hospitals, and public institutions.

4.2 Pollution Control
Water sprinkling, covered transport of materials, and waste segregation are mandatory. Environmental impact reports are required for large projects.

SECTION 5: AUTHORIZATION & ENFORCEMENT

5.1 Licensing & Approvals
Contractors must obtain operational licenses before commencing work. Any modification in project scope requires re-approval.

5.2 Penalties & Legal Action
Violations may lead to fines, blacklisting, or work suspension. Repeated offenses will result in cancellation of construction permits.
